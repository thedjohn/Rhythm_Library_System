The Concept

A smarter approach to drum pattern application

The Rhythm Library System is a simplification and expansion of an idea that's been floating around the drum world for years. The concept: using the solo pages from the middle of the Syncopation book for sequencing a select group of rudimental patterns or drumset patterns. The goal: creating a flow of patterns which conforms to the rhythmic architecture of the solo page — a wall of sound containing a rhythmic melody.

"In the past, when this idea was explained there were always eight patterns in a group — four patterns for the notes, and four patterns for the rests. Gordy discovered you could eliminate the rest patterns and still get the same results using just the note patterns — half note, dotted quarter, quarter note, and eighth note."

To make pattern application even easier, Gordy created a catalog — or Library — of simple two and four bar rhythmic phrases which combine only two or three note values at a time: Half Notes & Quarter Notes, Quarter Notes & Eighth Notes, Half Notes, Dotted Quarter Notes, & Quarter Notes, and more. These simpler combinations proved more useful and just as effective as applying all four patterns to more complicated rhythms.

The result is a system that is accessible to beginners, challenging for advanced players, and immediately useful for teachers looking for a structured approach to building student vocabulary.

About the Author

Gordy Knudtson — World-Class Drummer & Educator

Gordy Knudtson is a professional drummer, educator, and author with one of the most distinguished careers in modern drumming. He served as the drummer for the Steve Miller Band for more than thirty years — joining in 1987 and performing with them through 2021, including their induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2016.

Throughout his career Gordy has collaborated with Booker T. & the MGs, jazz pianist Ben Sidran, guitarist Roy Buchanan, and Cuban pianist Nachito Herrera. He began drumming at age 15 and turned professional at 17 — building a resume that spans studio work, international touring, and decades of performance at the highest level.

As an educator, Gordy headed the Percussion Department at McNally Smith College in St. Paul, Minnesota, where he created and directed the Drumset Diploma program. He also pioneered the development of Open/Close Technique — published in Percussive Notes Magazine and presented at PASIC 2003 alongside drum legends Joe Morello and Jim Chapin. Ed Shaughnessy described it as "an historically important form of technique that ranks with the Moeller system."
