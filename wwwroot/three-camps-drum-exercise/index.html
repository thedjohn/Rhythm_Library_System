What Is Three Camps?

Three Camps is a traditional rudimental snare drum exercise used in drum education for generations. In the Rhythm Library System, it becomes a rhythmic framework — a sequence of note values that you apply different sticking patterns to. Each time you change the sticking pattern, you hear a completely different musical phrase built from the same underlying rhythm.

How to Practice Three Camps

  1. Start slow with a click track — Begin at 60–70 BPM. Focus on accuracy before speed.
  2. Choose one sticking pattern — Select a single pattern from the GK 16ths group.
  3. Loop a single measure — Isolate difficult measures until they feel natural.
  4. Play through the full sequence — Play from beginning to end without stopping.
  5. Increase tempo gradually — Move up 5 BPM only when the pattern feels relaxed.
  6. Move it around the kit — Apply patterns to toms, cymbals, and kick.
