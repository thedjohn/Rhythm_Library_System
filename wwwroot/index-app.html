About the Method

What is the Rhythm Library System?

The Rhythm Library System is an 83-page drum instruction method by Gordy Knudtson designed to build fluent technique on both drumset and snare drum within a musical context. It uses common rhythmic notation to create hundreds of different combinations of sticking pattern ideas.

Whether you're working on solo vocabulary, hand independence, or rhythmic reading fluency, the Rhythm Library System gives you a structured, progressive approach — moving through four essential note values (eighth, quarter, dotted quarter, and half notes) with 24 drumset pattern groups and 16 rudimental pattern groups.

This free interactive app lets you hear and see every pattern in action — with adjustable tempo, click track, and real-time music notation so you can practice along at your own pace.

How It Works

Three steps to better drumming

1

Choose a Page

Select a rhythm exercise like Three Camps from the Page dropdown to load the notation sequence.

2

Choose a Pattern

Pick a GK 16ths sticking pattern from the Pattern dropdown. The pattern column shows the notes for each note value group.

3

Play and Practice

Press Play for a full run-through with count-in, or click any individual measure to loop it at tempo.
